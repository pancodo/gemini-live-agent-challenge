Kanun-i Osmani

بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم

This document, compiled under the auspices of Sultan Suleiman I, known among his subjects as al-Qanuni — the Lawgiver — sets forth the imperial statutes governing the administration of the Topkapı Palace and the conduct of all persons therein.

Topkapı records reference 400+ palace staff roles across 4 courtyard divisions

The palace shall be administered according to the four sacred courtyards, each representing a degree of proximity to the sovereign. The outermost courtyard, the Alay Meydanı, shall remain open to subjects bearing petition. The second courtyard, the Divan-ı Hümayun, shall be the seat of imperial council...

No person shall enter the third courtyard, the Enderun, without written permission of the Grand Vizier, sealed with the imperial tughra. Violations shall be punishable by no fewer than forty lashes and permanent exile from the capital.

On the matter of the Harem-i Hümayun: The imperial harem shall be governed entirely by the Valide Sultan, mother of the reigning sovereign. Under her authority shall serve no fewer than three hundred women of the household, divided by rank...

The Haseki Sultan, consort of the sovereign's affection, shall receive a stipend of no less than two thousand akçe per diem, and shall have the right to petition the sovereign directly, bypassing the Grand Vizier in matters pertaining to the household.

Craftsmen employed in the Nakkaşhane, the imperial design workshop, shall produce tiles, manuscripts, and decorative works exclusively for palace use. The master tileworkers, sourced from İznik, shall reside within the second courtyard during commission periods not exceeding six months...

The daily ritual of the sovereign shall proceed as follows: At the first call to prayer, the Hünkar shall be awakened by the senior page. The ablution ceremony shall be conducted with water drawn from the Bosphorus at dawn, carried in silver vessels by two designated pages...

The morning meal, prepared in the Matbah-ı Amire, shall consist of no fewer than twelve courses, prepared by kitchen staff numbering eight hundred souls. The tasting of all courses shall be performed first by the chief taster, in the presence of the head physician...

At the second prayer, the Divan shall convene in the second courtyard. The Grand Vizier shall preside. Petitions shall be read in order of province: Rumelia first, then Anatolia, then the Arab lands...

Regarding the Janissary Corps: The Yeniçeri Ağası shall command no fewer than five thousand troops in peacetime, garrisoned in the barracks adjoining the first courtyard...

The training of palace pages in the Enderun Mektebi shall encompass calligraphy, horsemanship, archery, music, and scripture. Pages shall be selected from the devşirme — the levy of Christian youths — between the ages of eight and eighteen...

Page 5 of 7

The Arz Odası, the Audience Chamber, shall be maintained at the highest standard. Its İznik tile panels, installed during the reign of Sultan Selim I, shall be cleaned monthly with rosewater. The cobalt pigment in these tiles, sourced from Persian Kashan, shall not be replicated with domestic materials...

The Mimar Başı, the Chief Architect, shall submit a written report on the condition of all structures no fewer than twice annually. Any structural deterioration in the fourth courtyard shall be reported to the sovereign personally...
